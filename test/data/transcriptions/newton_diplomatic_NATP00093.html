Prop 7 Demonstrated.

Lemma. If two bodys A, B, move uniformely ye oneother from ab to c, d, e, f,g, h, k, l, &c: in ye same time. Then are ye lines ac,bg, & cd,gh, & de,hk, & ef,kl, &c: as their velocitys p.q. And though they move not uniformely yet{illeg} are ye infinitely little lines wch each moment they describe, as their velocitys wch they have while they describe ym. As if ye body A wth ye velocity p describe ye infinitely little line \(cd=)/p×o in one moment, in yt moment ye body B wth ye velocity q, will describe ye line \(gh=)/q×o. For p:q::po:qo. Soe yt if ye described lines bee (ac=)x, & (bg=)y, in one moment, they will bee (ad=)x+po, & (bh=)y+qo in ye next.
